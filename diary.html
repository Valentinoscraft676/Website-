1.

Formulas ‐ Used In Woodworking Design

2 × r × π can be used to create garden tables that need substantial size for dining.

Because 15° × 24 is 360° it can be scaled for high end projects to utilise materials to 36° × 10 = 360°.

2.

Hexagons ‐ Practical Uses In Furniture Design

(B × H + 2 × 6) can be used for calculating how much material you need to fill the top of a coffee table.

For example, If you work in cm:

70 × 70cm = 4900cm2 ÷ 2 = 2,450cm2 × 6 = 14,700cm2

Oak: 10cm × 70 = 700cm2

14,700 ÷ 700 = 21 pieces of oak @ 10cm by 70cm

3.

Planning & Thicknessing Wood

I never had any experience a few years ago using precision machinery but I learnt well from a school woodworking class about the the process and logistics.

Purchased rough sawn timber
Square with circular saw
Measure thickness in mm
Check after each pass through using your hand as a guide for even smoothness
Then plan safely
Finally drawn a stick man "signature square symbol"

I am not an expert but I want to improve at it to save time and speed up production for customer and business orders.

The machine I use works in 0.5mm (half a mm intervals) so 0.5 × 20 ÷ 10 = 1cm thickness.

You have to take in mind the condition of your wood and any "knots" "burrs" or "warping" wood affects all pieces as they have to be equal.
